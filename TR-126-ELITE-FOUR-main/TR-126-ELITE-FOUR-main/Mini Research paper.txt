## Abstract

This project addresses the challenge of achieving reliable and efficient decision-making in decentralized environments where no central authority exists. Traditional systems rely heavily on centralized control, creating bottlenecks, single points of failure, and scalability limitations. To overcome these issues, we developed an AI-driven decentralized multi-agent decision system during the hackathon, where autonomous agents interact within a distributed environment and collectively arrive at optimal decisions using consensus mechanisms.

The proposed solution integrates reinforcement learning and distributed consensus algorithms, enabling agents to adapt dynamically based on local observations and peer interactions. Each agent independently evaluates possible actions while continuously updating its strategy through communication with neighboring agents. A lightweight consensus protocol ensures agreement without excessive communication overhead, improving system efficiency and robustness.

Experimental simulations demonstrated promising results. The system achieved faster convergence times compared to baseline decentralized approaches, even as the number of agents increased. Decision accuracy remained consistently high, indicating effective coordination among agents despite the absence of centralized control. Additionally, the system showed strong resilience to partial network failures and noisy inputs.

Overall, this work highlights the potential of combining AI with distributed consensus to enable scalable, fault-tolerant decision-making systems applicable to real-world domains such as smart grids, autonomous vehicles, and distributed robotics.
